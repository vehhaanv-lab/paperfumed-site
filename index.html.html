Plastic packaging has been the default for sixty years. Not because it was the best material. Because no one built a serious alternative at scale. We are.

India & plastic waste #1

India is the world's largest plastic polluter, generating 9.3 to 10.2 million tonnes of plastic waste annually, nearly one-fifth of global emissions.

Nature Journal  ·  University of Leeds  ·  2024
Actual recycling rate 12%

Of India's plastic waste is actually recycled after processing losses. The rest ends up in rivers, open landfills, and roadsides. Unaccounted for.

2–6 wk

Paper degrades completely

Under natural conditions, a paper-based bottle decomposes in two to six weeks. PET plastic takes an estimated four hundred to eight hundred years to break down in a landfill, and even then fragments into microplastics.

Global decomposition research consensus
68 vs 9%

Paper is actually recycled

The global paper recycling rate is 68%. The rate for plastic is 9%. Paper has more than seven times the real-world recycling efficiency of plastic at scale, backed by existing infrastructure in most markets.

UNEP Plastics Report  ·  FAO Paper Stats
60%

Lower carbon in production

Paper-based bottles produce an estimated 60% lower carbon footprint per unit compared to virgin PET plastic manufacturing, based on lifecycle analysis of paper bottle production including fiber sourcing and forming.

Paboco LCA  ·  Industry lifecycle benchmarks
0g

Zero microplastic residue

Paper bottles made with bio-based barrier coatings decompose without leaving microplastic residue. Standard PET bottles fragment into particles that persist indefinitely in soil, water systems, and human tissue.

Nature Medicine 2025  ·  Materials science research
FSC

Renewable by definition

Paper bottles are made from FSC-certified wood fiber or agricultural byproducts like sugarcane pulp. Both are genuinely renewable materials grown through natural photosynthesis, replacing fossil-derived petrochemical feedstock.

Forest Stewardship Council  ·  FSC certification framework
Jul 2022

India's ban is active

India banned 19 categories of single-use plastic in July 2022. Enforcement is tightening. EPR compliance mandates escalate through 2029. Large PET bottles must hit 85% reuse rates by 2029 under current regulations.

Plastic Waste Management Amendment Rules 2021  ·  MOEFCC
